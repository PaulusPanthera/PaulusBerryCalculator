Berry basics

The minimum you need to understand before farming seriously

  • Seeds are the planting input. Every berry uses a 2-seed or 3-seed recipe.
  • Seed flavors are spicy, bitter, sweet, dry, and sour. Each flavor can be plain or very.
  • Harvest Tools turn berries into seeds. One tool is consumed per berry and costs 350.
  • Not every berry is worth harvest tooling for seeds. Cheap 2-seed berries and the stronger 20h support berries matter most.
  • Yield means berries per plot. Growth time means how long the plant must stay alive before it can be harvested.
  • You need a watering can, seeds to start, and enough consistency to hit your watering and harvest windows.
Practical start: Buy your first seed stock from GTL, get comfortable with a few seed farms, then expand into berry or Leppa loops after you understand your timing.

Seed farming basics

Why some seed farms are much more realistic than others

  • 16h berries are the workhorse for straight seed farming. They are simple, quick, and easier to keep on schedule.
  • The 3× plain recipe and the 1 plain + 1 very recipe do different jobs. One favors plain sustainability, the other favors plain excess while keeping the loop alive.
  • 20h berries like Aguav and Mago produce more berries per plot, but they also put pressure on your very-seed economy.
  • If the buy price of a seed gets too high, farming your own seed can beat buying it. If it gets too low, buying can be the sane play.
  • The BerryDex breakpoint block exists for exactly that question: when is buying berries and harvest tooling them better than buying seeds directly?

Schedules

Routine ideas that normal humans can actually follow

16h routes Great when you can plant before sleep or before work and then clean up within the next day window.
20h routes Slightly more forgiving for harvest, but they usually want two touches and more discipline than pure 16h spam.
42h / 44h routes Better when you can commit to a repeating every-12-to-15-hour rhythm and want fewer full resets.
Leppa routines Either keep the schedule brain-off and stable, or accept more touchpoints because the money/day justifies it for you.
Most important schedule rule: plant everything first, then water. Your timer starts when you plant, so wide timing gaps across your fields create future pain.

Time investment

What a real daily routine tends to cost

  • Planting a full 156-plot character often lands around 3.5 to 4.5 minutes.
  • Harvesting often lands around 5 to 6.5 minutes.
  • Watering commonly lands around 2.5 to 3.5 minutes per pass.
  • Abundant Shrine encounters, mailing items, buying tools, and swapping characters are the hidden time tax that people underestimate.
  • One full account of three characters can easily become a 45+ minute routine when you include the real overhead.

General tips

Small habits that save a lot of money

  • Keybind your watering can or Harvest Tool if possible. Menuing slower than necessary adds up hard.
  • Set alarms until the farm becomes muscle memory. Missed timing is one of the biggest hidden profit leaks.
  • Do not overwater. Water once when the plot actually needs it.
  • If a route feels miserable to maintain, that is part of the route value. The cleanest money/day route is not always the best route for you.
  • If seed farming is beating the berry itself by a mile, question why you are farming the berry at all.
  • Use Home to spot strong routes, then open the deeper tabs only when you need the detailed breakdown.

Glossary

The words used around the calculator

Standard day The simplified money/day bucket used by the site: 1 day, 2 day, or 3 day.
Self-sustain The loop can keep going without recurring seed buys. Startup stock is ignored for this check.
Plain swap A very seed can be replaced by two plain seeds of the same color when the recipe still stays at three seeds or less.
Breakpoint The buy price where a berry plus one Harvest Tool becomes equal to the expected value of the harvested seeds.
Rebuy A route that still needs recurring buyback pressure in order to keep going.
Money/day first The current product philosophy: keep the defaults practical for normal users before layering a full /hr simulator on top.
